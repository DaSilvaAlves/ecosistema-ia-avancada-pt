# *Scirtothrips aurantii* in Algarve citrus

## Emerging pest — Characterisation, context and scientific response

**Frusoal InovCitrus** · Preliminary version 1.0 · 25/04/2026

---

## PAGE 1 — THE PEST

### Identification

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| Scientific name | *Scirtothrips aurantii* Faure |
| EPPO code | SCITAU <!-- F09 --> |
| Common name (English) | South African citrus thrips <!-- F09 --> |
| Family | Thripidae (Order: Thysanoptera) <!-- F09 --> |
| EU phytosanitary status | Quarantine pest — Annex II Part A of Commission Implementing Regulation (EU) 2019/2072 <!-- F12, F16 --> |
| EPPO list | A1 (priority quarantine list) <!-- F11 --> |
| EFSA reference | Pest categorisation — EFSA Panel on Plant Health, 2018 <!-- F11 --> |

### Morphology

*Scirtothrips aurantii* is a small insect. Adults measure approximately **1 mm**, which makes field detection particularly demanding in terms of monitoring methods. <!-- F01 -->

### Known distribution

| Region | Status |
|--------|--------|
| South Africa (origin) | Endemic <!-- F09, F11, F15 --> |
| Mediterranean basin — Spain (Huelva) | First EU detection: October 2020 <!-- F12 --> |
| Algarve, Portugal | Detected from 2022 <!-- F01, F02 --> |

The proximity in Huelva represents an ongoing regional vector of transmission, reinforcing the need for systematic monitoring of Algarve orchards close to the border.

### Hosts

Host tissues for *Scirtothrips aurantii* include:

- New vegetative growth
- Flowers
- Developing fruit <!-- F01, F11 -->

In citrus orchards, the pest preferentially attacks the tender emerging tissues during critical phases of the production cycle. In Andalusia, the pest has also been confirmed on persimmon, avocado and red fruits (strawberry, raspberry, blackberry, blueberry). <!-- F12 -->

## PAGE 2 — IMPACT AND BEHAVIOUR

### Symptoms on fruit

Feeding by *Scirtothrips aurantii* on tender tissues causes **surface scarring on the fruit, particularly around the calyx**, with possible progression to other areas of the rind. <!-- F01, F11 -->

| Type of damage | Commercial consequence |
|----------------|--------------------------|
| Marks and scars on the rind | Non-conformity in fresh-fruit markets <!-- F01, F11 --> |
| Deformations in attacked zones | Reduction in commercial fruit size |
| Premature drop of young fruit | Direct loss of yield |

### Economic impact

In conditions of uncontrolled infestation, the economic impact falls on three axes:

1. **Direct loss of marketable production** — non-conforming fruit redirected to processing at lower price
2. **Increased monitoring and treatment costs** — additional technical resources required
3. **Pressure on quality certifications** — including the Protected Geographical Indication (PGI) Citrinos do Algarve

The combination of these factors may compromise the commercial competitiveness of Algarve citrus in European markets, particularly in premium fresh-fruit segments.

### Gap in scientific knowledge

Despite the identification of the pest in 2022 in the Algarve, **there is still limited fundamental scientific knowledge about its behaviour under Portuguese Mediterranean conditions**. <!-- F01 -->

Current monitoring strategies are based on knowledge transferred from other regions and crops, which limits the precision of interventions and justifies the need for dedicated local research.

### Institutional framework

The Regional Directorate of Agriculture and Fisheries of the Algarve (DRAP Algarve) and the Directorate-General for Food and Veterinary Affairs (DGAV) have already promoted technical sessions dedicated to this pest, confirming its recognition as **a recent phytosanitary concern of Algarve citrus growing**. <!-- F02, F03 -->

The technical documentation produced by DRAP Algarve recommends, among other practices, the use of **chromatic sticky traps** and **systematic monitoring** of orchards. <!-- F02 -->

## PAGE 3 — THE RESPONSE: THREE-YEAR PROJECT

### The project

In February 2026, Frusoal launched **Frusoal InovCitrus** — a Research and Development centre dedicated to citrus growing — whose first scientific project is:

> **"Determination of the phenology and life cycle of South African citrus thrips in Algarve citrus orchards"** <!-- F01 paraphrase -->

### Project partners

| Partner | Role |
|---------|------|
| **Frusoal — Frutas Sotavento Algarve, Lda** | Promoter and in-house technical coordination <!-- F01 --> |
| **InnovPlantProtect (InPP)** | Scientific partner — agricultural entomology and scientific methodology <!-- F01 --> |
| **CCDR Algarve / Tavira Centre for Agricultural Experimentation** | Provision of trial fields <!-- F01 --> |
| **Frusoal member growers** | Provision of orchards for trials in real conditions |

### Frusoal's scientific track record

This is not Frusoal's first foray into scientific projects. Since **2018** Frusoal has been involved in the **Fruit Fly Protec** project on fruit flies — a direct antecedent of the work now institutionalised in InovCitrus. <!-- F06 -->

### Three-year design

| Period | Focus |
|--------|-------|
| 2026 | Methodological definition · monitoring infrastructure · first field campaign |
| 2027 | Second field campaign · interim analyses · first technical sheets for growers |
| 2028 | Third campaign · scientific consolidation · communication of the results |

### Trial sites

Trials will take place in three types of fields, ensuring representativeness:

1. **Direct Frusoal orchards** (Vila Nova de Cacela area)
2. **Member-grower orchards** (geographic spread across the Sotavento Algarvio)
3. **Tavira Centre for Agricultural Experimentation fields** — CCDR Algarve <!-- F01 -->

### Expected outputs

| Type of output | Beneficiaries |
|----------------|----------------|
| Operational technical sheets for growers | 65 Frusoal member growers + Algarve citrus sector |
| Scientific communications | National and European scientific community |
| Multilingual outreach materials (PT/EN/ES/FR) | Partner retailers · specialised press · European authorities |
| Applicable monitoring and control recommendations | Growers · agricultural technicians · DRAP Algarve · DGAV |

## PAGE 4 — ADDITIONAL INFORMATION

### Practical recommendations (summary of existing official sources)

> Monitoring and control of *Scirtothrips aurantii* in Algarve citrus orchards must follow the technical recommendations of the competent phytosanitary authorities — DRAP Algarve and DGAV.

| Recommended practice | Source |
|----------------------|--------|
| Chromatic sticky traps for detection and monitoring | DRAP Algarve <!-- F02 --> |
| Systematic orchard monitoring | DRAP Algarve <!-- F02 --> |
| Reporting of detections to competent regional authorities | DRAP Algarve / DGAV <!-- F02, F03 --> |
| Attendance of DGAV/DRAP technical sessions on the pest | DGAV / DRAP Algarve <!-- F03 --> |

> The specific recommendations resulting from the Frusoal InovCitrus three-year project will be progressively made available in the operational technical sheets published in the [Scientific Repository](/en/repository).
