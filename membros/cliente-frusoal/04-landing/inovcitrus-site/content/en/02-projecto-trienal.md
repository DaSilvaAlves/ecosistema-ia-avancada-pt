# Three-Year Project

## Phenology and life cycle of South African citrus thrips in Algarve citrus orchards

> The first scientific project of Frusoal InovCitrus, in collaboration with InnovPlantProtect and the Tavira Centre for Agricultural Experimentation (CCDR Algarve). <!-- F01 -->

---

## The pest

***Scirtothrips aurantii*** Faure — commonly known as **South African citrus thrips**. <!-- F09 official name -->

| Feature | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| Phytosanitary status | EU quarantine pest — listed in Annex II Part A of Commission Implementing Regulation (EU) 2019/2072 <!-- F12, F16 --> |
| EPPO list | A1 (priority quarantine list) <!-- F11 --> |
| First EU detection | Huelva (Spain), October 2020 <!-- F12 --> |
| Detection in Portugal | Algarve, from 2022 <!-- F01, F02 --> |
| Mediterranean precedents | Already identified in Huelva (Spain) before reaching Portugal <!-- F01, F12 --> |
| Size | Small insect — adults around 1 mm <!-- F01 --> |
| Tissues attacked | Young vegetative growth, flowers, developing fruit <!-- F01, F11 --> |
| Symptoms on fruit | Surface scarring on developing citrus fruit, particularly around the calyx, leading to commercial downgrading <!-- F01, F11 --> |

---

## Why this study matters

There is still **limited fundamental scientific knowledge** about the behaviour of this pest under Algarve conditions. <!-- F01 -->

The absence of robust local data on phenology and life cycle limits the precision of monitoring and control strategies, with direct consequences:

- Economic losses for member growers
- Risk to the competitive position of Algarve citrus in European markets
- Pressure on quality certifications, including the Protected Geographical Indication (PGI) for Citrinos do Algarve
- Reliance on reactive measures rather than data-driven agronomic planning

The three-year project aims to fill this gap with applied research carried out in real orchards.

## Scientific plan

| Dimension | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Duration | 36 months (2026–2028) |
| Main objective | Determine phenology and life cycle of *Scirtothrips aurantii* in Algarve citrus orchards |
| Trial sites | Frusoal orchards + member-grower orchards + fields of the Tavira Centre for Agricultural Experimentation (CCDR Algarve) <!-- F01 --> |
| Technical coordination | Frusoal in-house team + scientific team of InnovPlantProtect <!-- F01 --> |
| Expected methodology | Periodic field monitoring + identification of life-cycle stages + correlation with climatic factors and cultural practices |

## Project partners

### Frusoal — promoter

Frusoal is the largest Producer Organisation for citrus fruit in Portugal, with prior experience in scientific projects on pests — notably **Fruit Fly Protec** on fruit flies, ongoing since 2018. <!-- F04, F06 -->

### InnovPlantProtect — scientific partner

R&D centre dedicated to plant health, with expertise in agricultural entomology and a track record in collaborative European scientific projects. <!-- F05 -->

### CCDR Algarve / Tavira Centre for Agricultural Experimentation

Extension and field-trial partner, providing experimental infrastructure to the project. <!-- F01 -->

### Frusoal member growers

Member-grower orchards will be integrated into the trial design, broadening the geographical and varietal representativeness of the study.
