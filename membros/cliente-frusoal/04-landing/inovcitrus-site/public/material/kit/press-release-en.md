# Frusoal InovCitrus launches permanent Research and Development hub dedicated to Algarve citriculture

**Vila Nova de Cacela, Algarve — [DATE].** Frusoal — Frutas Sotavento Algarve, Lda, the largest Citrus Producers' Organisation in Portugal, has announced the launch of **InovCitrus**, a permanent Research and Development hub dedicated to Algarve citriculture. The hub will be headquartered in a facility under construction in Tavira and will serve as the operational centre for the technical and scientific effort of the regional citrus sector.

## First three-year project — *Scirtothrips aurantii*

The first scientific project of InovCitrus is a three-year programme, running from 2026 to 2028, focused on determining the phenology and life cycle of *Scirtothrips aurantii* in Algarve citrus orchards. The pest, native to South Africa, is classified as a quarantine pest by the European Union (Annex II Part A of Regulation (EU) 2019/2072) and was detected in Portugal in 2022.

The project is developed in partnership between **Frusoal** (promoter), **InnovPlantProtect** (scientific partner, based in Elvas) and the **Algarve Regional Coordination and Development Commission (CCDR Algarve)**, which provides the Tavira Agricultural Experimentation Centre as the field-trial site.

## Mission of InovCitrus

The hub is established with four articulated goals:

1. **Scientific knowledge** — produce applied knowledge on the life cycle and phenology of the pest under real Algarve conditions.
2. **Response to emerging pests** — develop monitoring protocols and operational recommendations for Frusoal's 65 member producers.
3. **Sustainability** — preserve the competitiveness and sustainability of regional citriculture in a context of recently introduced pests.
4. **Value enhancement** — consolidate Algarve citriculture as a sector underpinned by robust scientific evidence and solid institutional partnerships.

## Structure

InovCitrus operates under Frusoal's leadership. Frusoal brings together 65 member producers across approximately 1,500 hectares of orchard and 40,000 tonnes of annual production. Its commercial brands — **Gomo** (conventional) and **Biogomo** (organic) — supply the domestic market and export to Spain, France, Germany, the United Kingdom, Poland and Switzerland. Frusoal is GlobalG.A.P. and GRASP certified and holds the Protected Geographical Indication (PGI) "Citrinos do Algarve".

## Scientific and public communication

Across the three years of the project, InovCitrus will deliver a structured communication plan: technical fact sheets for growers, multilingual communications for the international sector audience, scientific publications and operational infographics. The detailed 36-month roadmap is documented in the institutional roadmap of the project.

## About Frusoal

Frusoal — Frutas Sotavento Algarve, Lda is the largest Citrus Producers' Organisation in Portugal. Founded in 1981, it brings together 65 member producers across roughly 1,500 hectares and 40,000 tonnes of annual production. It owns the Gomo (conventional) and Biogomo (organic) brands, holds GlobalG.A.P. and GRASP certifications and the Protected Geographical Indication (PGI) "Citrinos do Algarve". Frusoal exports approximately 25% of its production to European markets.

## About InovCitrus

InovCitrus is Frusoal's permanent Research and Development hub dedicated to Algarve citriculture. The headquarters is under construction in Tavira. The first scientific project, a three-year programme (2026-2028), is executed in partnership with InnovPlantProtect and CCDR Algarve.
